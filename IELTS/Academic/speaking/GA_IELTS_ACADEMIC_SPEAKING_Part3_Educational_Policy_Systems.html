📋 Discussion Format Overview

Part 3: Academic Discussion Guidelines

Duration: 4-5 minutes of interactive discussion

Format: Two-way conversation with examiner on abstract topics

Preparation: No preparation time - immediate responses required

Focus: Abstract ideas, opinions, analysis, and future speculation

💬 Discussion Questions

Educational Governance & Administration

Question 1
How should decision-making power be distributed between national governments, local authorities, and individual schools in educational systems?
💡 Consider: centralization vs. decentralization, local needs vs. national standards, administrative efficiency, democratic participation
Question 2
What role should parents and community members play in educational governance and policy-making decisions?
💡 Consider: stakeholder representation, expert knowledge vs. public opinion, democratic accountability, practical implementation
Question 3
How can educational systems balance the need for standardization with the desire for innovation and flexibility?
💡 Consider: quality assurance, pedagogical freedom, comparative assessment, adaptability to change
Question 4
What are the advantages and disadvantages of having education controlled by government versus private organizations?
💡 Consider: public service mission, market efficiency, equity concerns, quality control, ideological influence
Question 5
How might digital technology change the way educational systems are governed and administered in the future?
💡 Consider: data-driven decisions, automated processes, transparency, privacy concerns, digital divides

🎯 Band Score Guidance

Educational Policy & Systems Discussion Assessment Criteria

🗣️ Fluency & Coherence

Maintain sophisticated discourse when analyzing complex policy issues. Use advanced organizational structures to present multi-faceted arguments about educational governance and systematic reform initiatives.

📚 Lexical Resource

Employ specialized vocabulary from public policy, governance theory, and educational administration. Demonstrate precise terminology for policy analysis, institutional frameworks, and systemic evaluation.

⚙️ Grammatical Range

Display complex structures for discussing policy implications, comparative governmental systems, and hypothetical reform scenarios. Use sophisticated conditional and subjunctive forms for policy speculation.

🎯 Pronunciation

Maintain clear articulation of technical policy and governance terminology. Use appropriate stress patterns to emphasize key policy concepts and demonstrate confidence in academic policy discourse.

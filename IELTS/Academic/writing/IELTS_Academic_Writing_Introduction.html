IELTS Academic Writing

📊 Complete Practice Guide

Master both tasks of the IELTS Academic Writing test with comprehensive practice materials. From data description and visual analysis to academic essay composition, develop the analytical and writing skills needed to achieve your target band score with authentic academic task formats.

📋 Test Overview

⏰ Total Duration

60 minutes (no additional transfer time)

Key Information

  • Two mandatory tasks with different weights in final scoring
  • Task 1: Data description (150+ words minimum) - 1/3 of total writing score
  • Task 2: Academic essay (250+ words minimum) - 2/3 of total writing score
  • Time allocation: 20 minutes for Task 1, 40 minutes for Task 2 (recommended)
  • Four assessment criteria for each task with equal weighting
  • Band scoring from 1-9 in whole or half bands (averaged across criteria)
  • Academic contexts relevant to university study and professional development
  • No choice of questions - both tasks must be completed
  • Formal academic register required throughout both tasks

📊 Assessment Criteria

Both Task 1 and Task 2 are evaluated using four equally weighted criteria. Understanding these criteria is essential for achieving your target band score.

📝 Task Achievement (Task 1) / Task Response (Task 2) 25%
Task 1: Accurate data interpretation, clear overview, relevant details, appropriate length
Task 2: Address all parts of question, clear position, fully developed response, relevant ideas
🔗 Coherence and Cohesion 25%
Logical organization of information, clear progression of ideas, effective use of cohesive devices, appropriate paragraphing
📚 Lexical Resource 25%
Range and accuracy of vocabulary, precise word choice, natural and sophisticated usage, spelling accuracy
⚙️ Grammatical Range and Accuracy 25%
Wide range of grammatical structures, accuracy of complex sentences, error frequency and impact on communication

📊 Task 1: Academic Data Description

Requirements & Weight

150+ words minimum | 20 minutes recommended | 1/3 of writing score

Task 1 requires you to describe, summarize, or explain visual information such as graphs, charts, tables, diagrams, or maps. You must identify key features, trends, and patterns while maintaining an objective, factual tone throughout your response.

Visual Information Types

  • Charts & Graphs: Line graphs, bar charts, pie charts showing data trends and comparisons
  • Tables: Statistical data requiring analysis and comparison of figures
  • Process Diagrams: Step-by-step illustrations of natural or manufactured processes
  • Maps: Geographical changes over time or layout comparisons
  • Mixed Visuals: Combination of two different chart types for comparison

Common Task 1 Formats & Approaches:

📈 Line Graphs Trends over time
Focus: Changes and trends over time periods, comparing multiple lines
Key language: Rose, fell, fluctuated, peaked, remained stable
Structure: Overview → Major trends → Specific details and comparisons
📊 Bar Charts Category comparisons
Focus: Comparing different categories, identifying highest/lowest values
Key language: Significantly higher, considerably lower, approximately equal
Structure: Overview → Highest/lowest comparisons → Notable patterns
🥧 Pie Charts Proportions & percentages
Focus: Proportions, percentages, and relative sizes of segments
Key language: The majority, minority, nearly half, a quarter
Structure: Overview → Largest segments → Smaller segments and comparisons
📋 Tables Statistical data
Focus: Numerical data analysis, comparing rows and columns
Key language: Ranked highest, consistently lower, doubled between
Structure: Overview → Significant figures → Patterns and exceptions
🔄 Process Diagrams Sequential stages
Focus: Step-by-step process description, logical sequence
Key language: Initially, subsequently, finally, the process begins
Structure: Overview → Sequential stages → Key transformations
🗺️ Maps Geographical changes
Focus: Spatial changes, developments, layout modifications
Key language: Constructed, demolished, relocated, expanded
Structure: Overview → Major changes → Specific developments

Essential Task 1 Requirements

  • Include a clear overview identifying the main trends or key features
  • Select relevant information - don't describe every detail
  • Make meaningful comparisons between different data points
  • Write at least 150 words (aim for 170-190 for complete development)
  • Use objective, factual language without personal opinions
  • Organize information logically with clear paragraph structure

📝 Task 2: Academic Essay

Requirements & Weight

250+ words minimum | 40 minutes recommended | 2/3 of writing score

Task 2 requires you to write an essay responding to an academic topic, argument, or problem. This task carries twice the weight of Task 1 and demands sophisticated academic writing skills with well-developed arguments supported by relevant examples and evidence.

Common Academic Topics

  • Education & Learning: University systems, academic methods, online learning, skill development
  • Science & Technology: Research advances, artificial intelligence, medical breakthroughs, digital society
  • Social Issues: Urbanization, globalization, cultural change, demographic trends
  • Environment & Sustainability: Climate research, conservation policies, renewable energy, ecological impact
  • Economics & Development: International trade, economic policy, development strategies, employment
  • Culture & Society: Arts, media influence, social behavior, cultural preservation

Academic Essay Types & Approaches:

💭 Opinion Essays Argument & justification
Question phrases: "To what extent do you agree?", "Do you agree or disagree?"
Approach: Present clear thesis, develop supporting arguments with academic examples
Structure: Introduction + Thesis → Supporting arguments → Counter-argument → Conclusion
🔍 Discussion Essays Balanced analysis
Question phrases: "Discuss both views and give your opinion"
Approach: Analyze multiple perspectives objectively before presenting reasoned conclusion
Structure: Introduction → Perspective A → Perspective B → Personal view → Conclusion
⚖️ Advantages & Disadvantages Objective evaluation
Question phrases: "Discuss advantages and disadvantages", "Do benefits outweigh drawbacks?"
Approach: Systematic analysis of positive and negative aspects with evidence
Structure: Introduction → Advantages → Disadvantages → Evaluation → Conclusion
🛠️ Problem & Solution Analytical reasoning
Question phrases: "What are the causes and solutions?", "How can this be addressed?"
Approach: Identify root causes and propose evidence-based, practical solutions
Structure: Introduction → Problem analysis → Solution proposals → Implementation → Conclusion
❓ Two-Part Questions Multiple analysis
Question phrases: Two distinct questions requiring separate analysis
Approach: Address each question thoroughly with equal development and academic depth
Structure: Introduction → Question 1 analysis → Question 2 analysis → Conclusion

Essential Task 2 Requirements

  • Address all parts thoroughly with sophisticated analysis and reasoning
  • Present clear academic arguments supported by relevant evidence and examples
  • Demonstrate critical thinking with balanced evaluation of different perspectives
  • Write at least 250 words (aim for 280-320 for full development)
  • Use formal academic register with sophisticated vocabulary and structures
  • Organize ideas coherently with clear introduction, body, and conclusion

💡 Study Tips & Strategies

Before the Test

  • Practice both tasks regularly with Ga platform tools and timing
  • Familiarize yourself with all visual data types and essay question formats
  • Build academic vocabulary for data description and formal essay writing
  • Learn effective paragraph structures and sophisticated linking phrases
  • Use Ga platform assessment feedback to understand band score differences

During the Test

  • Read both tasks first, then start with Task 2 (higher weighting)
  • Spend 2-3 minutes planning each response before writing
  • Check word count requirements and exceed minimums safely
  • Monitor time carefully: 40 minutes Task 2, 20 minutes Task 1
  • Leave 3-5 minutes for checking grammar, spelling, and coherence

Task-Specific Strategies

  • Task 1: Include clear overview, select key features, make comparisons, avoid personal opinions
  • Task 2: Answer all parts fully, develop arguments with examples, maintain academic tone
  • Both tasks: Use varied vocabulary and complex sentence structures appropriately
  • Coherence: Use clear topic sentences and logical paragraph development
  • Time management: Prioritize Task 2 but ensure both tasks are completed

Key Success Factors

  • Consistent practice with Ga platform writing tasks and timing
  • Understanding of assessment criteria and band score requirements
  • Strong command of formal academic writing conventions
  • Ability to analyze data objectively and construct sophisticated arguments
  • Good time management skills under exam conditions

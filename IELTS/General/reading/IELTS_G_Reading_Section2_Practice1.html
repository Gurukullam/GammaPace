Passage 1: Office Safety Procedures Manual
HEALTHTECH SOLUTIONS - OFFICE SAFETY GUIDELINES

Fire Safety Procedures
All employees must familiarize themselves with the location of fire exits, which are clearly marked with green signs throughout the building. In case of fire alarm activation, employees should leave the building immediately via the nearest exit and proceed to the designated assembly point in the main car park. Do not use lifts during evacuation. Fire extinguishers are located on every floor near the stairwells and should only be used by trained personnel. Fire drills are conducted monthly on the second Tuesday of each month at 2:00 PM.

Accident Reporting
Any workplace accidents, no matter how minor, must be reported to the Health and Safety Officer within 24 hours. Incident report forms are available from the reception desk or can be downloaded from the company intranet. For serious injuries requiring medical attention, call emergency services immediately and then notify your line manager. A first aid kit is located in every department, and trained first aid officers are available during working hours.

Workplace Hazards
Keep walkways clear of obstacles such as boxes, cables, and personal belongings. Spills should be cleaned up immediately to prevent slip hazards. Report any damaged equipment or furniture to the maintenance department using the online request system. Heavy lifting should always be done using proper technique - bend your knees, keep your back straight, and ask for assistance if the item weighs more than 15 kilograms.

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
While not required for standard office work, PPE is mandatory in the warehouse and laboratory areas. Safety goggles and protective gloves are provided at the entrance to these areas and must be worn at all times. Visitors to restricted areas must sign in at reception and be accompanied by a member of staff. Hard hats are required in the basement storage area due to low ceiling height.

Emergency Contacts
Emergency services: 999
Health and Safety Officer: Extension 2847
Building Security: Extension 2100
Maintenance Department: Extension 2365
Passage 2: Computer Equipment Usage Guidelines
TECHCORP COMPUTING EQUIPMENT POLICY

Workstation Setup and Ergonomics
Proper workstation setup is essential for preventing repetitive strain injuries. Your monitor should be positioned at arm's length, with the top of the screen at eye level. Adjust your chair height so your feet rest flat on the floor and your knees are at a 90-degree angle. The keyboard and mouse should be positioned to allow your arms to remain relaxed at your sides. Take regular breaks every hour - stand up, stretch, and look away from the screen for at least 5 minutes.

Equipment Care and Maintenance
Clean your computer screen weekly using only approved cleaning solutions and microfiber cloths. Never use household cleaners or spray directly onto the screen. Keep food and drinks away from all computer equipment to prevent damage from spills. Shut down your computer properly at the end of each workday - do not simply turn off the power. Report any hardware problems immediately to the IT Support team via the helpdesk portal.

Data Security and Backup
All work files must be saved to the company network drive, not to your local computer. Personal files should not be stored on company equipment. Create strong passwords using a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols, and change them every 90 days. Never share your login credentials with colleagues. Automatic backups run every night at 2:00 AM, but employees are responsible for manually saving their work throughout the day.

Internet and Email Usage
Company internet should be used primarily for work-related activities. Limited personal use is permitted during break times, but downloading large files, streaming videos, or accessing inappropriate websites is strictly prohibited. All email communications should be professional and respectful. The company reserves the right to monitor internet and email usage. Social media access is blocked during working hours but available during lunch break from 12:00 PM to 1:00 PM.

Software Installation and Updates
Only IT-approved software may be installed on company computers. Requests for new software should be submitted through the IT portal with business justification. Automatic software updates are enabled and should not be disabled. If you receive notification of critical security updates, install them immediately. USB devices and external drives must be scanned for viruses before use and approved by the IT department.

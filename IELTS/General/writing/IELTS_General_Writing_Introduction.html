IELTS General Writing

✍️ Complete Practice Guide

Master both tasks of the IELTS General Training Writing test with comprehensive practice materials. From letter writing to essay composition, develop the skills needed to achieve your target band score with authentic task formats and professional assessment criteria.

📋 Test Overview

⏰ Total Duration

60 minutes (no additional transfer time)

Key Information

  • Two mandatory tasks with different weights in final scoring
  • Task 1: Letter writing (150+ words minimum) - 1/3 of total writing score
  • Task 2: Essay writing (250+ words minimum) - 2/3 of total writing score
  • Time allocation: 20 minutes for Task 1, 40 minutes for Task 2 (recommended)
  • Four assessment criteria for each task with equal weighting
  • Band scoring from 1-9 in whole or half bands (averaged across criteria)
  • Real-world contexts relevant to everyday situations and general interest topics
  • No choice of questions - both tasks must be completed

📊 Assessment Criteria

Both Task 1 and Task 2 are evaluated using four equally weighted criteria. Understanding these criteria is essential for achieving your target band score.

📝 Task Achievement (Task 1) / Task Response (Task 2) 25%
Task 1: How well you address all bullet points, appropriate tone and format, clear purpose
Task 2: Answer all parts of the question, present clear position, develop ideas sufficiently
🔗 Coherence and Cohesion 25%
Logical organization of ideas, clear progression, effective use of cohesive devices, appropriate paragraphing
📚 Lexical Resource 25%
Range and accuracy of vocabulary, appropriate word choice, natural and flexible usage, spelling accuracy
⚙️ Grammatical Range and Accuracy 25%
Variety of sentence structures, accuracy of grammar, error frequency and impact on communication

✉️ Task 1: Letter Writing

Requirements & Weight

150+ words minimum | 20 minutes recommended | 1/3 of writing score

Task 1 presents a situation requiring you to write a letter. You must respond to three bullet points in an appropriate tone and format. The task assesses your ability to communicate effectively in written English for practical, everyday purposes.

Letter Types by Formality

  • Formal letters: To unknown recipients, companies, institutions, authorities
  • Semi-formal letters: To known people in professional contexts (landlords, managers)
  • Informal letters: To friends, family, close acquaintances
  • Register consistency: Maintain appropriate tone throughout the letter

Common Letter Formats & Situations:

📄 Formal Letters Dear Sir/Madam
Situations: Complaints to companies, job applications, requesting information from organizations
Tone: Polite, respectful, professional
Closing: Yours faithfully (Sir/Madam) or Yours sincerely (named person)
🏢 Semi-formal Letters Dear Mr/Ms + surname
Situations: Writing to landlords, neighbours, work colleagues, course tutors
Tone: Polite but more personal, respectful
Closing: Best regards, Kind regards
💌 Informal Letters Dear + first name
Situations: Writing to friends, family members, close contacts
Tone: Friendly, personal, conversational
Closing: Best wishes, Love, Take care, See you soon

Essential Task 1 Requirements

  • Address all three bullet points with sufficient detail
  • Use appropriate letter format with greeting and closing
  • Maintain consistent tone throughout the letter
  • Write at least 150 words (aim for 170-190 for safety)
  • Organize content logically with clear paragraphs
  • Use appropriate register for the relationship and situation

📝 Task 2: Essay Writing

Requirements & Weight

250+ words minimum | 40 minutes recommended | 2/3 of writing score

Task 2 requires you to write an essay responding to a point of view, argument, or problem. This task carries twice the weight of Task 1 in your final writing band score, making it crucial for overall performance.

Common Essay Topics

  • Education: School systems, learning methods, university education
  • Work & Career: Employment trends, workplace changes, job satisfaction
  • Technology: Internet, mobile phones, artificial intelligence, automation
  • Environment: Climate change, pollution, conservation, renewable energy
  • Social Issues: Healthcare, crime, poverty, inequality, family structures
  • Lifestyle: Health, diet, exercise, leisure activities, urban vs rural living

Essay Question Types & Approaches:

💭 Opinion Essays To what extent do you agree?
Question phrases: "Do you agree or disagree?", "To what extent do you agree?"
Approach: State your position clearly and support with reasons and examples
Structure: Introduction + 2-3 body paragraphs + Conclusion
🔍 Discussion Essays Discuss both views
Question phrases: "Discuss both views and give your opinion"
Approach: Present both sides fairly, then provide your personal view
Structure: Introduction + View 1 + View 2 + Your opinion + Conclusion
⚖️ Advantages & Disadvantages Balanced analysis
Question phrases: "Discuss advantages and disadvantages", "Outweigh"
Approach: Present both positive and negative aspects objectively
Structure: Introduction + Advantages + Disadvantages + Conclusion
🛠️ Problem & Solution Causes and solutions
Question phrases: "What are the causes/problems and solutions?"
Approach: Identify key issues and propose practical solutions
Structure: Introduction + Problems/Causes + Solutions + Conclusion
❓ Two-Part Questions Multiple questions
Question phrases: Two separate questions requiring distinct answers
Approach: Address each question equally with dedicated paragraphs
Structure: Introduction + Question 1 answer + Question 2 answer + Conclusion

Essential Task 2 Requirements

  • Answer all parts of the question completely and relevantly
  • Present a clear position throughout your response
  • Support ideas with examples and explanations
  • Write at least 250 words (aim for 270-300 for development)
  • Use formal academic style appropriate for essay writing
  • Organize ideas logically with clear introduction and conclusion

💡 Study Tips & Strategies

Before the Test

  • Practice both tasks regularly with Ga platform tools and timing
  • Familiarize yourself with all question types and their specific requirements
  • Build vocabulary for common IELTS topics and formal/informal registers
  • Learn effective paragraph structures and linking phrases
  • Use Ga platform assessment feedback to understand band score differences

During the Test

  • Read both tasks first, then start with Task 2 (higher weighting)
  • Spend 2-3 minutes planning each response before writing
  • Check word count requirements and exceed minimums safely
  • Monitor time carefully: 40 minutes Task 2, 20 minutes Task 1
  • Leave 3-5 minutes for checking grammar, spelling, and coherence

Task-Specific Strategies

  • Task 1: Cover all bullet points, maintain appropriate tone, use proper letter format
  • Task 2: Answer all parts of question, develop ideas fully, provide relevant examples
  • Both tasks: Use varied vocabulary and sentence structures appropriately
  • Coherence: Use clear topic sentences and logical paragraph development
  • Time management: Prioritize Task 2 but ensure both tasks are completed

Key Success Factors

  • Consistent practice with Ga platform writing tasks and timing
  • Understanding of assessment criteria and band score requirements
  • Strong command of appropriate registers for different contexts
  • Ability to plan, develop, and organize ideas effectively
  • Good time management skills under exam conditions
